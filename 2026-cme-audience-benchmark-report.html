What the data says about reaching clinicians in 2026

The gap between the best- and worst-performing CME programs is no longer about content quality — it's about audience precision. Across 500+ organizations, programs that targeted on verified, intent-active audiences outperformed list-blast peers on every metric that matters: deliverability, enrollment, and credit completion.

This report establishes the 2026 baselines so you can see exactly where your programs sit against the field — and which levers move the needle fastest. Three findings stand out:

1. Data hygiene is the single largest driver of deliverability. Organizations refreshing their healthcare contact database quarterly saw inbox placement 24 points higher than those refreshing annually.

2. Intent timing beats list size. Smaller, intent-scored audiences out-enrolled larger untargeted ones by 8.4x — the in-market window matters more than reach.

3. Specialty segmentation compounds. Programs segmented by specialty and practice setting consistently outperformed, with the widest spread between top- and bottom-quartile results.

Methodology

How we built the benchmarks

Findings draw on aggregated, de-identified campaign data contributed by more than 500 healthcare organizations running accredited education between January 1, 2024 and January 1, 2025. In total we analyzed 1.8 million outreach sends, 143,255 enrollments, and 34 therapeutic areas.

All metrics are reported as medians with quartile spreads to avoid distortion from outlier programs. Organizations are grouped into three cohorts by audience-sourcing approach so you can benchmark against peers using a comparable model.

Benchmark 01 · Deliverability

Inbox placement separates the field

Deliverability is the first gate — an accredited invitation that lands in spam never gets the chance to convert. The spread between cohorts was the widest of any metric we measured.

What drives the gap

Authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), list hygiene, and refresh cadence explained the majority of variance. Programs that validated their healthcare contact database before each send avoided the hard-bounce penalties that drag sender reputation — and inbox placement — downward over time.

Benchmark to beat

Top-quartile programs sustained 32%+ inbox placement across the full year even on the hardest-to-reach lists. If you're below that, hygiene and authentication are your fastest wins.

The Playbook

Five moves to close the gap

The benchmarks point to a consistent playbook. None of these moves require more budget — they require more precise audiences and faster timing.

  1. Refresh your healthcare contact database quarterly

    Audience decay is the silent killer of deliverability. Quarterly NPI re-verification kept top programs 24 points ahead on inbox placement.

  2. Authenticate every sending domain with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC

    Authentication is table stakes. Programs without full authentication never reached top-quartile inbox rates regardless of content.

  3. Layer intent data on top of verified audiences

    Verification gets you to the right person; intent gets you there at the right moment. Together they drove the 8.4x enrollment lift.

  4. Act on intent signals within 24 hours

    The in-market window is short — a median of 72 hours. Build workflows that trigger outreach the same day a signal fires, not the next campaign cycle.

  5. Segment by specialty and practice setting

    A single message to all clinicians underperforms. Segmented programs showed the widest top-quartile advantage in the study.
