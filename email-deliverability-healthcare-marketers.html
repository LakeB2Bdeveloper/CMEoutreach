Executive Summary

Inbox placement is now a marketing priority

For Continuing Medical Education (CME) providers, email remains the most effective channel for promoting accredited activities, driving registrations, and maintaining engagement with healthcare professionals (HCPs). Yet even the most relevant educational invitation loses its value if it never reaches the recipient's inbox.

Today's mailbox providers use increasingly sophisticated algorithms to decide whether an email earns inbox placement. Sender reputation, email authentication, recipient engagement, and database quality all shape the outcome. Healthcare marketers face an added challenge: physician and HCP data changes constantly as clinicians move between hospital affiliations, practice settings, specialties, and roles.

As a result, many organizations unknowingly send to outdated or invalid addresses, driving up bounce rates and eroding sender reputation. Over time, even legitimate educational emails begin landing in spam.

The shift

Improving deliverability is no longer just an IT concern. It is a strategic marketing priority that directly affects registration rates, learner acquisition, and campaign ROI.

This whitepaper outlines the factors that influence deliverability and the practical steps healthcare marketers can take to improve inbox placement, supported by verified, continuously maintained healthcare data from CMEIntent.

The Landscape

Why deliverability is getting harder in healthcare

Healthcare professionals receive hundreds of emails each week from hospitals, medical societies, pharmaceutical companies, technology vendors, journals, recruiters, and educational organizations. Mailbox providers respond with stricter filtering, so only trusted and relevant messages reach the inbox.

Unlike many B2B sectors, healthcare databases decay quickly. Physicians change hospitals, join larger health systems, move into academic roles, retire, or update their professional addresses. Practice acquisitions and health-system mergers accelerate the churn.

Continuing to mail aging records leads to a predictable downward spiral:

Rather than expanding mailing lists indefinitely, successful healthcare marketers focus on smaller, verified, and highly engaged audiences.

The Framework

The four foundations of strong deliverability

Deliverability improves when these four practices work together rather than independently.

1

Email Authentication

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC verify that mail genuinely comes from your domain, preventing spoofing and building sender credibility.

2

Data Hygiene

Removing invalid addresses, duplicates, role-based inboxes, and bounced recipients protects reputation and cuts wasted sends.

3

Email Validation

Validating addresses before every send identifies inactive, risky, or invalid accounts and sharply reduces bounce rates.

4

Audience Relevance

Targeting by specialty, clinical interest, geography, or role lifts opens and clicks, reinforcing positive sender reputation.

Building a healthy healthcare marketing database

Database quality directly influences campaign performance. Effective marketers establish ongoing data governance instead of relying on periodic cleanup projects. Key best practices include:

  • Remove hard bounces immediately
  • Validate physician email addresses regularly
  • Update specialty and professional credentials
  • Eliminate duplicate records
  • Suppress long-term inactive recipients
  • Monitor engagement trends
  • Refresh contact data throughout the year
  • Treat the database as a living asset

Organizations that invest in database quality consistently achieve stronger inbox placement and higher campaign engagement.
