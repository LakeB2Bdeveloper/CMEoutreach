Executive Summary

From who an HCP is to what they need now

Healthcare marketing has traditionally relied on demographic and firmographic data: specialty, geography, credentials, and practice type. While these attributes help define who a healthcare professional (HCP) is, they reveal very little about what that individual is actively interested in today.

Intent data changes that equation. By analyzing digital behaviors such as content consumption, clinical research activity, webinar participation, guideline reviews, and continuing medical education (CME) engagement, marketers can identify professionals who are actively exploring specific therapeutic areas or educational topics.

The core idea

Instead of broadcasting the same message to an entire specialty, organizations can prioritize the audiences showing current interest, improving relevance, engagement, and return on investment.

This whitepaper explains how HCP intent data is generated, how it differs from traditional healthcare data, and how CME providers can use behavioral insights to build more effective marketing campaigns.

The Foundation

Understanding HCP intent data

Intent data captures behavioral signals that indicate an individual's current interests or information needs. Rather than relying solely on static profile information, it reflects actions taken across digital channels over time. For healthcare marketers, these behaviors may include:

Reading clinical articles Downloading guidelines Webinar participation Searching for therapies Visiting specialty sites Exploring CME programs Engaging educational content

A single interaction may not reveal much. But when multiple activities occur within a defined timeframe, together they create a stronger signal that an HCP is actively researching a topic.

These patterns can be aggregated and scored to help marketers identify audiences more likely to engage with relevant educational content. For CME providers, that means promoting activities to professionals already demonstrating interest in the subject matter.

High-intent audiences can then be activated across email campaigns, programmatic advertising, LinkedIn, educational webinar invitations, sales outreach, and account-based marketing. Instead of targeting every physician in a specialty, marketers focus resources on those showing measurable interest today.

The Payoff

Why intent data matters for CME marketing

Accredited education succeeds when it reaches professionals at the right moment. Traditional audience selection often assumes every physician in a specialty shares the same educational priorities. In reality, learning needs evolve continuously with clinical practice, emerging evidence, guideline updates, and patient populations. Intent data aligns outreach with current interests.

  • More relevant educational invitations
  • Higher email engagement
  • Improved webinar attendance
  • Increased CME registrations
  • Better campaign efficiency
  • More informed sales conversations
  • Reduced marketing waste
  • A better experience for HCPs

Intent data should complement, not replace, traditional attributes such as specialty, geography, credentials, and practice setting. Combining behavioral insights with trusted healthcare data provides a more complete view of the audience.
