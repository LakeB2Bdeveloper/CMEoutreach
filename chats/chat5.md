Good question. The left media slot fills **half the strip's width** and uses `fit: cover` — so the GIF is scaled to fill the area and center-cropped, meaning exact dimensions aren't critical, but matching the slot's shape avoids awkward cropping.

**Recommended:**
- **Aspect ratio:** ~16:9 (landscape) — the left half is roughly square-to-landscape depending on screen width, and 16:9 crops cleanly.
- **Resolution:** **1280×720** (or 1600×900 for sharper on large screens). That's plenty for a half-width strip and keeps file size manageable.
- **File size:** aim for **under ~3–5 MB** so the page stays snappy — GIFs balloon fast. If it's heavy, an **MP4/WebM** loop looks far better at a fraction of the size.

A couple of notes:
- On mobile the strip stacks, so the media sits on top at full width with a **min-height of 260px** — a landscape GIF still reads well there.
- Keep the **important action centered**, since the edges may crop at certain widths.

If your GIF is more of a tall/portrait clip, tell me and I can switch the slot to `fit: contain` (shows the whole frame, no crop) or adjust the column proportions. Also — image-slot accepts GIFs/MP4s directly; just drag it onto the left half whenever ready.
